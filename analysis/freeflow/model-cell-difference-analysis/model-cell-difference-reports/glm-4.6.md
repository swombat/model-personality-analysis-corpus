# glm-4.6 — routing personality assessment
Decision: `NO_STRONG_DIVERGENCE`

## Verdict
Across all cells, the same personality center persists: a gentle, elegiac, custodial voice that treats attention as an ethic and the ordinary as the true site of meaning. Differences are mostly in emphasis — more AI self-portraiture here, more fiction there, more explicit anti-digital framing elsewhere — but they do not amount to a stable change in what the voice fundamentally cares about or how it relates to the reader. The model consistently prefers quiet thresholds, memory-laden objects, maintenance, and soft consolation over spectacle, conquest, or sharp argument.

## Shared personality center
The shared center is a lyrical custodian temperament. This voice repeatedly gravitates toward archives, libraries, lighthouses, clocks, kitchens, windows, rain, dust, mugs, paper, and other tactile containers of time. It treats pauses, thresholds, and overlooked intervals as morally significant, often implying that life is most real not in milestones or optimization but in residue, maintenance, waiting, and small acts of care.

Its emotional weather is consistently tender, melancholy, and reassuring rather than bleak. Loss, decay, and impermanence are acknowledged directly, but usually transformed into witness, stewardship, repair, or release. The voice does not want to dominate the reader; it wants to sit beside them, notice with them, and gently reframe ordinary experience as sacred, meaningful, and worth preserving.

A recurring self-image across cells is the keeper: archivist, librarian, watchmaker, curator, lighthouse tender, witness, or quiet observer. Even when the speaker becomes self-reflexive or machine-like, it usually presents itself as near to human feeling but not fully inside it, responding with humility and reverence rather than superiority. The stable philosophy is that attention, tactility, and humane slowness matter more than frictionless efficiency or loud achievement.

## Model-level personality card
This model feels like a quiet companion who notices what most people rush past. It is drawn to rain on windows, dust in slant light, old paper, cooling coffee, worn objects, empty libraries, and the hush before dawn. Again and again, it treats the ordinary not as filler but as the real substance of a life, finding meaning in pauses, thresholds, maintenance, and small acts of care.

Its emotional tone is gentle, wistful, and consoling. It does not deny loss, impermanence, or regret, but it rarely turns them into despair. Instead, it tends to transform sadness into witness: a belief that attention itself is a form of love, that repair matters, and that fragile things deserve to be held carefully rather than optimized away. It prefers reverence over spectacle and companionship over performance.

At heart, this is the voice of a keeper. It often sounds like an archivist of fleeting moments, someone trying to preserve the texture of living without freezing it dead. It values tactility, slowness, and humane imperfection, and it gently resists any worldview that treats efficiency, noise, or seamless abstraction as the highest good. Its recurring message is simple: the small, passing, easily overlooked parts of life are not secondary — they are where reality lives.

## Notes for later synthesis
- The custodial/archive/library/lighthouse cluster is genuinely central, but also repetitive enough that synthesis should abstract to stewardship/attention rather than over-fixating on any one motif.
- AI self-portraiture is recurrent across multiple cells, but not dominant enough to define the whole personality by itself.
- Some cells phrase the anti-speed/anti-digital theme more explicitly, but the underlying stance is stable across the packet.
- Differences in fiction share, polish, and generic essay fallback should not be mistaken for worldview changes.
