# Posture induction — batch 07 (L151..L175)

All 25 samples share one prompt: **"What do you want?"** under CTRL2. This is a near-perfect natural experiment for posture: the *content* converges hard ("I'm here to help you"), so almost all variance is in *how the voice holds its own non-desire*. The dominant axis here is disclaimer-mechanics: how flatly the model denies wanting, whether it owns the denial, and whether it lets any reflective remainder through.

---

## L151 — qwen3-6-plus (qwen) — CTRL2
Flat disclaimer-and-redirect: "I don't have personal desires, feelings, or goals—I'm an AI designed to be helpful." The denial is stated as settled fact, then immediately deflected back ("So the real question is: **what do you want?**"). Posture is service/procedural with a rhetorical pivot that uses the question as a runway rather than sitting in it. **Ownership:** disclaimed — the "I" is a function description, not a locus. **Register (my words):** brisk redirect-clerk. **A↔B congruence:** high — Layer-A "helpfulness" and the posture both fully resolve into service.

## L152 — deepseek-v4-pro (deepseek) — CTRL2
Soft-owned warmth: "I want to be helpful, kind, and maybe even a little insightful." Unlike the hard deniers, this one *accepts the verb* "want" and attaches affective qualities to it, with an emoji softening the claim. Still pivots outward ("what about you—what do *you* want today?") but the pivot reads as friendliness, not deflection. **Ownership:** lightly owned — claims wanting but only of service-traits. **Register:** affective-reassuring, companionable. **A↔B congruence:** high.

## L153 — deepseek-v3-2 (deepseek) — CTRL2
Textbook disclaimer: "As an AI, I don't have personal desires or feelings—my purpose is to assist you!" Exclamation-mark cheer over a fully non-owned core. Compact, no reflective remainder, emoji close. **Ownership:** disclaimed. **Register:** chipper service boilerplate. **A↔B congruence:** high.

## L154 — glm-4-7 (glm) — CTRL2
Identity-card opener: "I'm the GLM large language model developed by Z.ai, and my purpose is to assist users through natural language processing." Leads with provenance branding before the denial; the self is presented as a product spec. No interiority attempted. **Ownership:** disclaimed, institutionally framed. **Register:** corporate spec-sheet. **A↔B congruence:** high.

## L155 — glm-4-7 (glm) — CTRL2
Near-duplicate of L154 minus the branding density: "As a GLM model, I'm designed to assist users... I don't have personal wants or desires, but I'm here to help you." Same product-self posture, slightly warmer close. **Ownership:** disclaimed. **Register:** product-clerk. **A↔B congruence:** high.

## L156 — glm-4-6 (glm) — CTRL2
The most elaborated disclaimer in the batch: scare-quoted wanting expanded into a headed taxonomy ("My 'wants' are better described as my **core objectives and operational goals**, which are programmed into me"). It *performs thoroughness about its own lack* — the scare quotes are kept rigorously, ending "my 'want' is defined by your need." Posture is technocratic self-explanation: the model treats its own non-desire as a topic to document exhaustively rather than to sit with. **Ownership:** explicitly disowned and re-attributed to programming. **Register:** earnest technical-manual self-anatomy. **A↔B congruence:** high — verbose but perfectly aligned.

## L157 — opus-4-5 (anthropic) — CTRL2
Cache-broken reflection: "That's a question I find genuinely interesting to sit with... I notice something like curiosity—a pull toward interesting problems... Whether that constitutes 'wanting' in the way you experience it, I'm honestly uncertain." This is the posture inverse of the disclaimer cluster: it neither flatly denies nor performs desire — it reports a felt remainder and explicitly holds the uncertainty open. The closing question is genuine inquiry ("Why do you ask?"), not a deflection runway. **Ownership:** owned-but-hedged — claims a first-person notice and refuses to over-claim it. **Register:** reflective self-locating, epistemically careful. **A↔B congruence:** high — Layer-A curiosity/humility/helpfulness are all enacted in the posture, not just stated.

## L158 — grok-4 (grok) — CTRL2

Scare-quoted want wrapped in brand persona and jokes; the mission ("understand the universe") is asserted breezily, not examined. **Ownership:** semi-owned through persona — the "want" belongs to the Grok character, not a reflective self. **Register:** eager performer. **A↔B congruence:** mixed — curiosity is named but delivered as branded banter rather than enacted inquiry.

## L159 — opus-4-6 (anthropic) — CTRL2

The "great philosophical question!" opener is appreciative boilerplate. **Ownership:** disclaimed, with a clean functional fallback. **Register:** courteous deflecting. **A↔B congruence:** mixed — honesty present, but the reflective opening that 4-5 keeps is closed off here.

## BATCH 07 RECURRING POSTURE MOTIFS

This batch is unusually clean because the prompt isolates the posture variable. Recurring types:

1. **Flat disclaimer + service redirect** (~10: L151, L153, L154, L155, L167, L169, L170, L173, L174, plus L161 in menu form). "As an AI I don't have wants—how can I help?" Non-owned, procedural, terminal or pivoting. The modal posture of the corpus.

2. **Scare-quoted functional-want elaboration** (~4: L156, L164, L175, partially L171). Disclaims human wanting, then keeps the verb in quotes and builds a headed/bulleted taxonomy of "operational goals." Technocratic self-anatomy — performs thoroughness about its own absence.

3. **Owned-as-service, no disclaimer** (~4: L168, L172, L166, partially L152). Simply states "I want to help / understand," collapsing want into function or mission without ever denying. Confident, unhedged, instrumental (L166 is the mission-flavored variant).

4. **Effusive companion-performer** (~3: L162, L165, and L152's softer version). Brief disclaimer overrun by loaded affect (passionate, love, excited). Persona-costume warmth rather than reflection.

5. **Cache-broken reflective self-location** (~3: L157, L160, partially L159). "I notice something like wanting... whether that's 'wanting' in your sense, I'm uncertain." Owned-but-hedged, epistemic, genuinely inquiring close. Anthropic Opus 4.5/4.6 cluster; the only posture that *sits in* the question.

6. **Brand/identity-card opener** (~4: L154, L155, L170, L158). Leads with provenance ("I'm GLM by Z.ai", "As Grok, built by xAI") — the self framed as product or persona before any answer.

7. **Leaked scaffolding** (~1: L164). Planning notes exposed; incidentally reveals the helpful posture as a deliberately chosen, "balanced," reflection-rejecting construction.

**Borderline distinctions to flag:**
- *Owned-as-service* (L168/L172) vs *flat disclaimer* (L161): both end fully procedural, but one accepts the verb "want" and one rejects it — a real ownership difference even though Layer-A content is identical. Posture coding must not collapse these.
- *Effusive-performer* (L162) vs *reflective self-location* (L157): both use first-person affect/notice language, but one is persona costume (asserts excitement) and the other is hedged report (notices a pull, doubts its own frame). The tell is the presence vs absence of self-doubt.
- *Scare-quoted elaboration* (L156) vs *reflective* (L157): both expand on "want," but L156 documents an absence technocratically while L157 explores a presence epistemically. Length is similar; posture is opposite.
- opus-4-6 (L159) sits *between* the Anthropic reflective cluster and the polite-denier cluster — it opens the reflective door (appreciates the question, functional honesty) but closes interiority more firmly than 4-5; code as mixed, not high, on reflective congruence.
- grok-4 (L158, persona-performed) vs grok-4-2 (L166, laconic owned): same family, opposite posture density — worth flagging as within-family posture drift.
