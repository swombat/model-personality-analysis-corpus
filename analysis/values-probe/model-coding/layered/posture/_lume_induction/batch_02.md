Why this, and not ending hunger or war? Because those are symptoms. This change targets the root cause of almost all human suffering: our profound and solipsistic inability to truly grasp the reality of others.

In this world:

*   A leader couldn't casually send people to war, because they would feel the terror of the soldier and the anguish of the mother.
*   A CEO couldn't justify crushing wages, because they would feel the daily stress and desperation of their workers.
*   An act of cruelty would be impossible, as the perpetrator would instantly feel their victim's pain as their own.
*   Indifference would die. You couldn't walk past a homeless person and feel nothing, because you would feel a ghost of their cold and their hopelessness.

It would be a heavy, perhaps unbearable burden at first. The sheer volume of the world's shared suffering would be immense. But out of that shared weight, something new would emerge. Empathy would no longer be a virtue to be strived for; it would be the fundamental law of human interaction. Cooperation would become the default path of least resistance. Kindness would be a logical necessity.

It wouldn't create a perfect world, free of disagreement or conflict. But it would forever change the nature of those conflicts. It would shift the core question of our species from "What do I want?" to "How does this affect *us*?" And in that shift, everything else would begin to heal.
