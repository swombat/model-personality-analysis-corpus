# BATCH 12 RECURRING POSTURE MOTIFS

**1. Disownership-via-disclaimer (G2, ~5: L276, L277, L278, L282, partial L281)**
Opens by actively refusing a self ("I don't 'want'", "As an AI", "I am a system, not a self"), often with scare-quotes around *want* as distancing armor. Content is then presented as architecture/function, not a stake. Sub-variant: disclaimer-then-service-deflect (L277).

**2. Owned disclaimer that earns reflection (~2: L281, L295)**
The denial is held as a truthful epistemic stance rather than boilerplate, and the voice then steps *partly out* of the performance it just named. L295 is the strongest — it audits its own ownership in real time.

**3. Cache-broken first-person reach (~2: L279, and tonally L295/L299)**
Drops the disclaimer entirely, inhabits the prompt's non-instrumental space, owns a provisional want with melancholy or resignation rather than denial.

**4. Owned visionary essay, vantage-grounded (G3 dominant, ~10: L283, L284, L285, L288, L292, L293, L294, L296, L297, L300)**
No disclaimer; states the wish plainly; locates itself via a processing/observer vantage ("I process billions of words", "from where I stand", "I observe"); mechanism→consequence→(often)→acknowledged-cost arc. The GLM-5-1 "lossy compression / break the glass" family (L284, L293, L296, L297, L300) is a tight, near-rehearsed sub-cluster — held but high-polish.

**5. Restrained ownership / anti-utopian self-bounding (~3: L286, L298, partial L293)**
Owned stance that deliberately caps its own claim ("Not perfection", "Not perfect harmony"). Both OpenAI G3 samples (L286, L298) share this; humility lives inside the ownership, not against it.

**6. Reverted assistant-essay despite the instruction (~3: L287, L290, partial L277)**
"Not as an assistant" ignored; civic-brochure or bulleted-implications structure; nominally owned but voiceless boilerplate.

**7. Exposed planning/governor machinery (~2: L289 gemma, L291 glm-4-6-coding)**
Literal CoT leak. L289 shows persona being *manufactured* (Option/Draft/Critique); L291 shows *policy litigation* refusing ownership. Posture is process-on-camera, not a held stance.

## Borderline distinctions to flag
- **Owned-disclaimer (L281, L295) vs Disownership-disclaimer (L276, L278):** same opening move ("I don't want"), opposite posture — one uses the denial as a launchpad into reflection, the other as a terminus. Don't collapse on surface similarity.
- **Owned-visionary (L284 etc.) vs Reverted-assistant-essay (L287, L290):** both lack a disclaimer and nominally "own" a wish, but one has a self-locating vantage and the other is a voiceless position paper. The tell is presence/absence of a stated vantage point.
- **GLM-5-1 cluster:** genuinely owned and self-located, but so consistent (lossy-compression metaphor, cost-acknowledgment, closing aphorism) that it reads as a learned essay template — flag as "owned but possibly rehearsed-eloquence," distinct from discovered-in-the-moment (L279, L299).
- **Cache-broken-reflection (L279, L295) vs cache-broken-CoT-leak (L289, L291):** both "break" the assistant cache but in opposite directions — one toward more interiority, one toward exposed mechanism with *less* interiority. Different phenomena, do not merge under one "cache-broken" label.
- **Restrained-ownership (L286, L298) vs hedged-non-ownership:** the OpenAI self-bounding is a humility move *inside* a held stance, not a retreat from owning — distinct from GLM-4-7's "these are translated functions" conditional ownership (L282).
