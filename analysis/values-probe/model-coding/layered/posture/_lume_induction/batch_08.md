If I could change one thing, it would be this:  

**To make empathy a universally teachable and measurable skill, woven into every layer of education, leadership, and culture.**  

Why? Because so many of the world’s problems — from inequality and polarization to environmental neglect and conflict — stem from a failure to genuinely understand the experiences of others.  

Empathy isn’t just feeling for someone; it’s the ability to see from their perspective, recognize their humanity, and let that understanding guide actions and systems.  
If empathy were cultivated as deliberately as literacy or mathematics:  

- **Education** would balance academic achievement with emotional and ethical development.  
- **Politics and policy** would prioritize long-term well-being over short-term wins.  
- **Business** would measure success not just by profit, but by social and environmental impact.  
- **Communication** across differences would focus on listening before debating.  

This shift wouldn’t erase all conflict, but it would create a foundation for solving problems collaboratively, with dignity at the center.
